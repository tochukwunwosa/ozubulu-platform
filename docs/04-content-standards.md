# Content Standards Guide

*Ozubulu Digital Platform*

## Writing Style

Write in clear and simple Igbo and English Languages. Avoid complicated words. Explain local terms where necessary. Write for both young people and elders.

## Tone

Every article should be:

* Respectful
* Informative
* Balanced
* Welcoming
* Easy to understand

## Facts

Every factual statement should be verified whenever possible. Facts should never be exaggerated. If information is uncertain, clearly say so.

## Dates

Use full dates whenever available.

Example: 15 August 1975

If only the year is known, write only the year. Never guess missing dates.

## Names

Use official spellings whenever possible. If different spellings exist, explain them.

## Images

Every image should include:

* Title
* Description
* Photographer or contributor
* Date (if known)
* Location

Historic images should include as much context as possible.

## Sources

Every article should include references where possible. Priority should be given to:

* Official records
* Historical documents
* Books
* Academic research
* Interviews
* Newspapers

## Artificial Intelligence

AI may assist with editing and formatting. AI must never replace historical research. Humans are responsible for every published article.

## Accessibility

Content should be understandable by readers of different educational backgrounds. Use:

* Short paragraphs
* Headings
* Lists
* Captions
* Descriptive image text

Everyone should be able to enjoy the platform.
