# Editorial Policy

*Ozubulu Digital Platform*

## Purpose

The Ozubulu Digital Platform exists to preserve, document, and share the history, culture, people, and development of Ozubulu. This policy explains how information is collected, verified, published, corrected, and preserved.

Our commitment is simple:

> Accuracy before speed. Truth before popularity. Community before personal opinion.

## Editorial Principles

Everything we publish must follow these principles:

* Be truthful.
* Be respectful.
* Be fair.
* Be properly verified.
* Be written in clear and simple language.
* Be useful to the Ozubulu community.

## Sources of Information

Information may come from:

* Interviews with elders
* Traditional institutions
* Town Union records
* Church records
* School records
* Government publications
* Books and academic research
* Newspapers and magazines
* Historical documents
* Personal photographs and archives
* Community submissions

Whenever possible, every article should reference its sources.

## Verification Policy

No historical fact should be published simply because someone says it is true. Information should be confirmed through at least one of the following:

* Two independent and reliable sources.
* An official document or publication.
* Confirmation from the appropriate traditional, religious, educational, or community authority.
* Historical evidence that can be independently verified.

When verification is not possible, the information will be marked as:

* Unverified
* Oral Tradition
* Under Review
* Disputed

We will never present uncertain information as established fact.

## Editorial Authority

For Version 1, Nwosa Tochukwu Chinemelu is the Founder, Editor-in-Chief, and final editorial authority. Responsibilities include:

* Reviewing all submissions.
* Verifying information.
* Publishing approved content.
* Correcting errors.
* Protecting the integrity of the platform.

Future editorial responsibilities may be shared with an Editorial Board appointed by recognised Ozubulu community institutions.

## Community Contributions

Everyone is welcome to contribute. Community members may submit:

* Historical information
* Family stories
* Old photographs
* Videos
* Documents
* Corrections
* Suggestions
* Event information

Every submission will be reviewed before publication. Submitting information does not guarantee it will be published.

## Corrections Policy

We believe every publication can be improved. If an error is discovered:

* The correction will be investigated promptly.
* Verified errors will be corrected within 30 days whenever possible.
* Major corrections may include an editor's note explaining the update.
* False or misleading information will be removed.

We welcome corrections because they strengthen the platform.

## Handling Disputes

Some topics may have different versions of history. Examples include:

* Traditional leadership
* Village boundaries
* Family histories
* Chieftaincy titles
* Historical events
* Land ownership

When disagreements arise:

* We will gather available evidence.
* We will consult recognised authorities where appropriate.
* We will seek multiple independent sources.
* If the matter remains unresolved, we will clearly state that different accounts exist rather than choosing one without evidence.

The platform exists to preserve history, not to settle disputes.

## Privacy Policy

We respect personal privacy. We will not publish:

* Private contact information without permission.
* Personal information about living individuals without consent.
* Sensitive family matters.
* Defamatory or malicious content.
* Content that may endanger individuals.

Requests to remove personal information will be considered fairly and promptly.

## Photography and Media

Whenever possible:

* Credit will be given to photographers and contributors.
* Permission should be obtained before publishing privately owned photographs.
* Historical photographs should include dates and context where known.

We aim to preserve, not exploit, community heritage.

## Artificial Intelligence

Artificial Intelligence (AI) may assist with:

* Writing drafts.
* Improving grammar.
* Organising information.
* Translating content.
* Formatting articles.

AI will never be treated as a historical source. Every historical fact must be reviewed and verified by a human editor before publication.

## Political and Religious Neutrality

The platform welcomes all members of the Ozubulu community. It will not endorse political parties or candidates. Religious institutions may be documented as part of Ozubulu's history and community life, but all denominations will be treated with fairness and respect.

## Commercial Content

Businesses may be listed in the directory. Editorial content will never be influenced by sponsorship or advertising. Sponsored content, where accepted, will always be clearly identified.

## Content Updates

History continues to be written. Articles will be reviewed and updated as new information becomes available. Each page should display:

* Date published
* Date last updated
* Sources consulted
* Version history (where applicable)

## Long-Term Preservation

This platform is being built for future generations. Our goal is not simply to publish information today, but to preserve it for decades to come. Every article, photograph, document, and story contributes to the shared history of Ozubulu.

## What Success Looks Like After One Year

By December 2027, we will consider Version 1 successful if:

* At least 200 pages contain verified and properly sourced information.
* At least 100 historical photographs or documents have been preserved in the digital archive.
* At least 50 community submissions (stories, corrections, photographs, or documents) have been received and reviewed.
* The platform receives at least 10,000 unique visitors during its first year.
* Visitors come from both Nigeria and the Ozubulu diaspora across multiple countries.
* The platform receives formal recognition, endorsement, or collaboration from at least one recognised Ozubulu community institution, such as the Traditional Institution, Town Union, or a registered community association.
* No confirmed factual error remains uncorrected for more than 30 days after it has been verified.

Success is not measured only by traffic. Success means preserving our history, serving our people, and earning the trust of the community.

## Version 1: What Is Realistic

Our vision is much bigger than Version 1. The first release is only the foundation. Before launch, every feature will be evaluated based on:

* Availability of verified information.
* Importance to the community.
* Time required to build and maintain.

If a section is not ready, it will wait until a future release. We would rather publish fewer excellent pages than many incomplete ones. For this reason, features such as a full News platform, Business Directory, Interactive Map, Oral History Archive, and other advanced tools may be introduced after the initial launch.

Quality will always come before speed.

## Ownership and Continuity

Version 1 of the Ozubulu Digital Platform is founded, designed, and maintained by Nwosa Tochukwu Chinemelu. He is responsible for the technical development, platform maintenance, hosting, and editorial decisions during the initial phase of the project.

The long-term goal is for this platform to become a permanent community asset that continues to serve Ozubulu for generations. After launch, discussions may begin with recognised community bodies, including the Traditional Institution, Town Union, village representatives, and other stakeholders, about creating a sustainable governance structure for the platform.

Whether the platform is eventually handed over to a community organisation or continues to be maintained by its founder, every decision will be guided by one principle:

> The history, culture, and identity of Ozubulu must remain protected, accurate, and accessible for future generations.
