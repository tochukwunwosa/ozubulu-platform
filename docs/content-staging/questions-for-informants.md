# Questions for Community Informants

Working list of what's still needed to continue entering real content into the
Ozubulu Digital Archive. Organized by community/topic so it can be split up
and sent to whoever can answer each part. Report answers back per question
number (or just paste replies inline) and they'll be entered into Sanity.

## Amakwa — Villages (kindreds)

We already have the names of the 5 kindreds: Ezi-Amakwa, Umudezim, Umuowelle,
Okwutu, Umuezekwe Ofufe. For each one, if known:

1. Igbo spelling/name if different from the above (some of these may already
   be the Igbo form — confirm).
2. Meaning of the name, if there is a known origin story or translation.
3. Brief history — when/how it was founded, notable founding families.
4. Geography — roughly where it sits within Amakwa, any landmarks.
5. Boundaries — what it borders (other kindreds, other communities, roads,
   rivers, etc.).

## Traditional leadership — lineage questions

8. Ezeifeukwu Oruche I was Obi of Amakwa around 1904 (worked with Paramount
   Chief Edward Nnubia Anadu to bring Christianity to the area). The current
   Igwe of Ozubulu is HRH Igwe Fidelis Nnamdi Oruche, also from Amakwa, also
   surnamed Oruche. Are these two from the same ruling dynasty/lineage, or is
   the shared surname a coincidence? If related, how (e.g., direct
   descendant, same ruling house, unrelated families)?

9. Chief Odenigbo Ezeani of Eziora (1906) worked with Rev. Fr. Cluny and Rev.
   Fr. Victor Duhaze to establish the Catholic church at Eziora. The current
   Obi of Eziora holds the title "Odenigbo IV." Is the current Obi a
   descendant of, or in the same title-lineage as, the 1906 Chief Odenigbo
   Ezeani? If so, is there a known list of who held the "Odenigbo" title
   between I and IV?
